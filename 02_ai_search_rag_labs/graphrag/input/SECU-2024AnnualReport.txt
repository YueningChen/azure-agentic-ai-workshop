Report of the Chair
THERE IS A DIFFERENCE
State Employees' Credit Union (SECU) is a member-owned cooperative grounded in the philosophy of People Helping People® and steadfast in its not-for-profit mission of service. One of the things I appreciate most is how SECU lives out its mission - not just talking the talk, but also walking the walk - working to make a positive difference for our more than 2.8 million members and the communities in which they live. SECU is about people. It's about strong and trusted relationships we've built with members since 1937 when the Credit Union was formed. At SECU, there is a difference. As your SECU Board Chair, I am pleased to share more about that difference and how it is reflected in the work accomplished over the past fiscal year.
With any member-owned cooperative, the value of listening to those you serve cannot be overstated. Hearing from you helps direct our work to best meet your financial needs through all phases of life. This feedback is important in guiding our direction with the products, services, and tools to help improve your financial lives. To help us communicate with you even better, we implemented more tools over the past year. We now have the ability to email with members who have an active email address on file, and we recently began the rollout of a new chat feature with our SECU Mobile App. We also implemented a new member survey tool, which allows us to gather continuous feedback from our various service channels. We are now able to gain more insight into member satisfaction, which I'm pleased to report remains very high at SECU. This same tool was used earlier this year to survey members on proposed changes
to our Board Election Policies and Procedures. More than 33,000 of you responded with overwhelming support of the changes, and we appreciate your engagement to help guide our decisioning on these important issues. For more in-person connection, we've been traveling around the state hosting roadshows and member listening sessions to share information and to hear from SECU volunteers and members about topics that are important to you. We look forward to continuing these in the future.
We're hearing you and responding with ways to keep more money in your pockets, where it belongs. Over the last year, our Board voted to eliminate numerous fees, including fees for stop payments, overdraft transfers, NSF representments, verifications of deposit, and returned items. We are pleased to share that SECU's fees remain among the lowest in the country when compared to our credit union peers. We've also been working to increase deposit rates so you can earn more on your savings. For the first time ever, we offered a special 9-month share term certificate (STC), and many of you are enjoying the benefits of this new product. When comparing SECU's deposit rates to other institutions, our STCs and Money Market Share Account offer top of market rates. Member deposits show that you are taking advantage of these great rates, and we are pleased to see this upward trend in deposits.
Much work has also been accomplished in the lending area, as we strive to offer the best possible loan rates to serve all members. The previous one-price-for-all strategy prohibited

While the Board chose to start with five tiers of rates based on credit scores, the Board consistently monitored the impacts of this model and over the past year we compressed those rates to an improved three-tier model. Not only are we able to offer excellent rates regardless of a member's credit score, but we are also continuing to serve members who have little to no lending options in the marketplace. This is a member-first approach, and we are excited to see members who were borrowing elsewhere come back to SECU for their lending needs. Our good news for lending didn't stop there. We also rolled out a discount loan program for qualifying current and retired State of North Carolina employees on closed-end consumer loans, adding even more value for our base membership.
Other areas of positive change included the refresh of our website, www.ncsecu.org. You asked for improved navigation, a more streamlined and intuitive login process, better search functionality, and more tools, and your Credit Union delivered. You also requested card lock and unlock features, and I'm happy to report that this valued benefit is now available on SECU debit and credit cards. Finally, one of our most member-requested products is nearing the launch stage - the highly anticipated SECU rewards credit cards, which will allow card holders to earn cash back, or accumulate points rewards that can be redeemed for gift cards, merchandise, or travel. The Cooperative Difference
Born out of compassion for others and the desire for positive change, State Employees' Credit Union was formed by 17 individuals on June 4, 1937, in the basement of the Agriculture Building in Raleigh, North Carolina. After watching their co-workers, family, and friends being taken advantage of by dishonest lenders, our founders knew there was a better way - one that could make a real difference. In a bold move, they pooled their resources, a total of $437, and created a trusted place where fellow state employees and their families could save and borrow money at fair and equitable rates for all.

Listening To Members ...
Roadshow in Lenoir, North Carolina
The trusted relationships built with members since 1937 are foundational to the success of SECU. As a member-owned cooperative, gathering feedback from members is central to helping guide the Board and SECU leadership to make decisions that best serve the needs of all members. To enhance member communications, SECU implemented even more feedback tools over the past year. The Credit Union now has the capability to email members who have an active email address on file, helping to provide more information through e-newsletters, important notices, and marketing messages highlighting new and improved offerings. Over 1.2 million members are currently receiving these emails, and the goal is to expand that distribution to even more of SECU's 2.8 million members.
SECU also began a graduated rollout of a new chat feature with the SECU Mobile App to complement its online and direct communications with members. Chat is currently available 8:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m. Monday-Friday with plans to expand availability in the coming year.
A new member survey tool was also implemented that provides continuous feedback through SECU's various service channels. In addition to gaining greater insight into member satisfaction, this tool has been helpful with gathering input on specific topics such as the proposed changes to SECU's Board Election Policies and Procedures that were approved by the Board earlier this year. More than 33,000 members responded to the survey with overwhelming support of the changes, which helped guide the Board's decision.
With a desire for greater in-person communication, SECU's Board and leadership traveled around the state hosting roadshows and member listening sessions for SECU volunteers and members. The engagements provided an opportunity to share information and hear about topics that are top of mind for them. The conversations have been invaluable and plans for more of these events are already underway for next year. Just as it was in the beginning, cultivating trusted member relationships is vitally important to SECU.

... and Responding to Their Needs
Listening to what members want and need from their financial cooperative is key, but responding to meet those needs is the true difference maker. Members spoke and SECU responded this past year with better savings and lending rates, fewer fees, new and improved offerings, and greater convenience.
SECU's Board made great strides in increasing deposit rates to help members earn more on their savings. Just before fiscal year-end, a new 9-month Share Term Certificate (STC) was made available for a limited time, with many members taking advantage of this special offering and highly competitive rate. In comparison with other peer financial institutions, the Credit Union's STC and Money Market Share Account rates are at the top of the market.
Since the beginning, SECU has been grounded in the philosophy of People Helping People®.
Members also requested more competitive lending rates and the Board answered the call with an improved lending strategy to offer members the best possible loan rates regardless of their credit score. Moving away from the one-price-for-all model for auto and consumer loans and transitioning to a five- tier model in the previous year produced positive results; however, after monitoring the impacts, the Board made further improvements by compressing the model to three tiers. They also rolled out a new discount loan program that offers a 0.50% discount* for qualifying current and retired State of North Carolina employees on closed-end consumer loans.
*To be eligible for this discount, members must be a current full-time or retired employee of the state of North Carolina (including public school employees) and members' state payroll or retirement benefits from a plan administered by the North Carolina Retirement Systems division of the Department of State Treasurer must be on direct deposit with SECU. Additional terms and conditions apply.

What the Numbers Say
Staying true to its purpose, SECU has always operated with a member-first approach. By listening and responding, the cooperative's membership has consistently grown year over year. SECU now serves more than 2.8 million members, an increase of nearly 70,000 from the previous fiscal year. While work continues to enhance and expand service offerings, as well as delivery and communications channels, fiscal strength also stays top of mind as outlined in SECU's longstanding mission statement.
MISSION STATEMENT
To be the trusted provider of financial services to every eligible member and to enhance the value of their lives and financial well-being while maintaining our fiscal strength.
SECU remains a safe and sound not-for-profit cooperative, which is reflected in the audited fiscal year financials available at www.ncsecu.org. Grounded solely in North Carolina, approximately one in four North Carolinians are members. The Credit Union has a branch presence in all 100 counties, with 275 offices from Murphy to Manteo, offering a local touch, which is complemented by SECU's website and mobile app, the 24/7 Member Services Support Center, ASK-SECU Voice Response System, and nearly 1,100 CashPoints® ATMs statewide.

MEMO SECU Caring House
While philanthropic work has long been an extension of SECU's mission, the State Employees' Credit Union Board of Directors took a bold leap of faith in 2004 to expand the scope of what SECU could accomplish by establishing the SECU Foundation. Created to help identify and address large scale community issues in the areas of education, housing, healthcare, and human services, SECU Foundation now stands as the largest charitable organization in the credit union industry. The funding concept that was established twenty years ago continues to be one of the most unique - funded by SECU
members who have an active checking account and contribute through the reallocation of their $1 monthly maintenance fee. The concept is referred to as The Power of a Dollar, and thanks to the generosity of SECU members, the work that has been accomplished by SECU Foundation is nothing less than remarkable. To date, SECU Foundation has committed over $300 million in scholarships, grants, and loans to benefit North Carolinians in all 100 counties. Happy Anniversary to SECU Foundation and congratulations on 20 years of impactful funding for the people and communities of our great state!
